Agentic AI Mediated Reality

DELICIOS

Delegation of Decision-Making to Autonomous Agents in Socio-Technical Systems (DELICIOS) is an international research project funded by FWO (grant number G054919N) and FNR (grant number 12635165), carried out between 2019 and 2023 by Ghent University, Vrije Universiteit Brussel, and the University of Luxembourg.

My role in this project was to investigate whether and how mediated-reality technologies (e.g., VR/AR) influence users' delegatory behaviors toward agentic AI. A series of human-computer interaction experiments was carried out to explore different design choices of agentic AI (e.g., how should the agent look like in its appearance, or should the agent proactively build rapport with users?). Based on the experiment results, we proposed a theoretical framework modelling the delegatory relationship between human users and agentic AI, and from there, a few design guidelines were derived for LLM agents nowadays.

Containing the research of four years within this brief webpage is quite challenging and can also be tedious. I would recommend interested readers to check my thesis directly, particularly on Chapters 1 and 2 where a more detailed discussion on the research question is given there. But if I had to extract one single message from the thesis I would say:

The design of the user interface for agentic AI nowadays needs careful consideration. As LLMs rapidly enable software with near-human level intelligence and behaviors, the delegation between users and AI is more and more resembling interpersonal delegation, which, as often discussed in the research of economics and management, inherently has various problems and is susceptible to many factors. These problems may also arise in user-AI delegation, and thus, the user interface design should try to mitigate the impact.
